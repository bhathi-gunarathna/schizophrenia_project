Why Multimodal?

fMRI captures where brain abnormalities occur spatially; EEG captures when they occur temporally. Together they form a richer diagnostic signature, reducing false positives and capturing patterns that individual modalities miss.

How Fusion Analysis Works
🔗
Cross-Modal Fusion
Feature vectors from fMRI and EEG pipelines are concatenated and passed through a joint classification head.
📐
Spatial + Temporal
The model exploits complementary information — structural location (fMRI) and timing dynamics (EEG).
⚡
Flexible Input
Model gracefully handles single-modality input, falling back to the respective unimodal classifier if needed.
📈
Higher Accuracy
Fusion consistently outperforms single-modality baselines with higher sensitivity and specificity.
