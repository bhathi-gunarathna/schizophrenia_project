About SchzoFusion

A final-year research project developing a multimodal AI platform that integrates fMRI and EEG neuroimaging data to provide early, objective screening support for schizophrenia.

Deep Learning Neuroimaging Healthcare AI Multimodal Fusion
🎯

Project Mission

SchzoFusion aims to reduce the diagnostic delay associated with schizophrenia by providing clinicians and researchers with a fast, non-invasive, AI-assisted screening tool. By fusing complementary neuroimaging modalities — functional MRI and EEG — the platform captures both the spatial and temporal dimensions of schizophrenia-related brain abnormalities in a single unified analysis.

Platform Overview
🧠
fMRI Module
3D CNN analyses BOLD signal patterns to detect spatial schizophrenia biomarkers in functional connectivity maps.
📈
EEG Module
LSTM classifier analyses temporal brainwave dynamics across frequency bands to identify oscillatory abnormalities.
🔬
Fusion Module
A joint classification head fuses spatial and temporal feature vectors for enhanced multi-modal screening accuracy.
Research Background

Schizophrenia is a severe psychiatric disorder affecting approximately 1 in 100 people globally. Despite its prevalence, clinical diagnosis remains challenging due to symptom overlap with other conditions and reliance on subjective clinician assessment. The average delay from first symptoms to confirmed diagnosis is 1–2 years.

Neuroimaging studies have identified consistent structural and functional differences in the brains of individuals with schizophrenia — including altered prefrontal-limbic connectivity on fMRI and abnormal gamma-band oscillations on EEG. SchzoFusion operationalises this research by training deep learning models on these biomarkers to deliver rapid, objective screening support to research workflows.

This project is developed as a Final Year Project (FYP) at the Institute of Information Technology (IIT) and does not constitute a medical device or clinical diagnostic tool.
